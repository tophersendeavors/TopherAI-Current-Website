How Much Does an AI Receptionist Cost?

A complete pricing guide — what's included at each tier, what drives cost, and how to choose the right plan.

Quick Answer

AI receptionist pricing ranges from $99/month for basic call answering to $1,500+/month for fully custom enterprise systems. Most small and mid-size businesses land on a mid-tier plan ($297–$697/month) that covers lead qualification, appointment booking, SMS follow-up, and CRM integration. Compare this to a human receptionist at $48,000–$70,000/year fully loaded, and the ROI becomes clear immediately.

What Drives the Cost

Call volume and minutes

Most subscriptions include a monthly minute allotment. A plan covering 500 minutes costs significantly less than one covering 5,000 minutes. Estimate your average call duration (typically 2–4 minutes for AI interactions) and multiply by expected monthly call volume to size your plan correctly.

Integration complexity

Connecting to Google Calendar or Calendly is standard and usually included. Syncing with a custom CRM, practice management software, or multi-step automation workflow adds cost — either in setup fees or a higher-tier subscription.

Customization depth

A generic "answer and qualify" workflow is less expensive to build than a highly customized flow for a law firm that needs case-type routing, conflict-of-interest screening, and jurisdiction-specific question sets. Custom workflows increase both setup fees and monthly cost.

Number of locations or agents

Enterprise clients with multiple office locations, multiple phone numbers, or multiple agent calendars require additional configuration. Most platforms price these as add-ons or higher-tier plans.

What Should Be Included in Your Plan

Before signing any AI receptionist contract, confirm the following are included:

  • 24/7 call handling — not just business hours
  • Custom conversation flow specific to your industry and business, not a generic script
  • Real-time calendar booking — not just message capture for a human to follow up on
  • SMS confirmation and follow-up after each interaction
  • CRM or lead sheet delivery — call data should go somewhere actionable automatically
  • Call recordings and transcripts — essential for QA and training
  • Bilingual support if you serve Spanish-speaking callers

AI Receptionist Cost vs. Human Receptionist Cost

The total annual cost of a human receptionist — salary, benefits, payroll taxes, paid time off, and turnover costs — typically runs $48,000–$70,000. An AI receptionist mid-tier plan runs $3,564–$8,364/year. The AI covers more hours, handles more calls simultaneously, and delivers more consistent lead qualification. For most businesses, the ROI calculation takes less than five minutes.

How to Choose the Right Plan

Match your tier to your actual call volume and business complexity. Start by estimating your monthly inbound call count and average call duration. If you receive 100 calls/month at 3 minutes each, a mid-tier plan with 400–600 included minutes is sufficient. If you're running paid advertising and expecting 500+ calls/month, size up.

Ask for a 30-day trial or a live demo before committing. The conversation quality — how natural, responsive, and accurate the AI sounds — matters as much as the feature list.
